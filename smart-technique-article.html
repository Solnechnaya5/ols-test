Project Management From Preparation to Retrospectives

Project Preparation

Before the start of the project, the team performs several steps for its preparation. For successful projects, especially fixed-price projects, it's important to have a determined time and scope of work that the team must complete. Ensuring the product aligns with all business needs is where Project Management comes into play.

Steps Before and During Planning

Let's take a closer look at the common steps before and during the planning phase. In general, there are several steps before we begin the project. These are the main ones:

  • Initiation: Describing the business flow of the project.
  • Planning: Utilizing the SMART technique to identify key objectives.
  • Preparing for the Project.
  • Monitoring the Performed Work.
  • Finalizing the Project.

Understanding SMART Goals

What about the SMART technique? We can describe it using the letters as follows:

  • SSpecific: A goal is effective when it's specific.
    • M Measurable: Quantifying your goals.
      • A Achievable: Goals should be realistic.
        • R Relevant: Why are you setting the goal that you're setting?
          • T Time-bound: You should know when the realistic goal can be achieved.
Ask yourself these questions when performing planning and be honest. This is the way to reach your goals.

The Importance of Retrospectives

During the work on the project, it's crucial to look back and review the progress made and make updates to the working process flow. We call it a retrospective.

Retrospectives in Agile and Scrum

First of all, a retrospective is a tool in Scrum planning used especially for the team. It's a meeting held at the end of a sprint where the team discusses the issues they encountered, what improvements can be made for a smoother process flow, and what new methodologies they used or can be used in the next sprint. It's a valuable process for the team to analyze their work, discuss it, and make improvements in the next sprint.

Leveraging Agile, Scrum/Kanban Planning, and Retrospectives

Techniques such as Agile, Scrum/Kanban planning, and Retrospectives are invaluable in successful project management. During the process review, you can identify improvements that can be applied to your workflow, increasing the effectiveness and speed of the team's work.
