Project Planning

Project Lifecycle: MYTH OR MUST?

Project Management is an effective tool for any type of project. It helps to keep all deadlines, approved estimations, and expected results. Customers can be sure that the performed work will be done on time.

Sometimes during the working process the situations may occur when the team is not in the approved time schedule because of the hard level of the task or may some unexpected earlier situations. That's life, everything can happen. How in this situation Project Management can help?

Let's overview to how the best organize the project flow step-by-step:

Before the start of the project, the team performs several steps for its preparation. For successful projects especially fixed-price projects it's important to have a determined time and scope of work that the team has to do. “The product must meet all business needs, so Project Management is necessary”

Success in project management isn't about racing to the finish line; it's about orchestrating each step with precision, turning chaos into harmony, and ensuring that every note contributes to the symphony of success.
Oleg - Project manager at
One Logic Soft

During the preparation of the project, we have to perform several useful steps in its planning to avoid runoff of the budget, team overtime, and overall stressful situations.

There are main ones: Initiation - the phase where we describe the business flow of the project, describe user stories, and confirm the main key points which should be done.

The next one is Planning, the step where you plan all necessary activities and tasks which should be performed during the project. For this Project Managers are using several techniques such as SMART, Agile, Scrum or Kanban.

* Usually, the method of performing planning depends on the Project Requirements and can include more than one method of planning during one project. *

After the Planning we can move to the most interesting and changeable parts of the working flow - Project Execution.

The project will never be successful if we don't correctly implement and monitor the ideas and tasks which we described during the Planning phase. You should focus on filling the gaps if they may occur, manage the team and review the performance of the work to be on set. Be flexible in changing the process flow if it's applicable to get the success.

The Monitoring and Controlling involves reporting on the processes that have been done by the team, by collecting timesheets, reports, and review documentation. Analyzing the scope of performed work and implementing new features. What a spectacular period, isn't it?

From one spectacular period to another - Closure of the Project. Okay, you may think that this is the time for the party, but no, let's look at what is coming before the project ending party. The answer is Review. As a project manager you have to review the performed work, make a team review, prepare all significant documentation and document your analysis of the project. Which will help you to improve the work in the next challenges.

Now you know how we build our project workflow, but we didn't mention about the tools which are mandatory for it. Let have a look together.

WHAT ABOUT THE TOOLS?

We have said before that PM is the process that is performed by one or several members of the team to connect the requirements from the client and convert them into the tasks that the team will perform.

To avoid some blocks that may occur during the work during the preparation of the project, the team has daily calls to discuss all issues, questions, and suggestions.

Aso after each Sprint the team has a retrospective, which is the process that has been discovered especially for the team to discuss the methodologies that have been used, ideas for the next sprint, what they will use in the next one, and what is better to leave in the sprint that had already gone.

Daily meetings, sprints, performed tasks, and documentation that are prepared during the process help clients to be updated about the flow of the project and be sure that everything is on track.

During this process, we share with the Client all necessary documentation such as: flow diagrams, time reports, sprint review, technical solutions, and other which is required.

There may be situations that require data and necessary help from the Client. In this way we escalate the problem to the hire level and discuss the possible solutions together. Everything to perform great projects at the end.
